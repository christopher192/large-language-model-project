Class 13 Exercises

Sutta Number: DN30.2
Pali: samaṃ pādaṃ bhūmiyaṃ nikkhipati, samaṃ uddharati, samaṃ sabbāvantehi pādatalehi bhūmiṃ phusati.
English Translation: He puts down [his] foot evenly on the earth. He lifts [his foot] up in a balanced manner. He touches the earth evenly with the entire sole of [his] foot.

Sutta Number: MN20
Pali: kiṃ nu kho ahaṃ sīghaṃ gacchāmi? yaṃnūn’āhaṃ saṇikaṃ gaccheyyan’ti. so saṇikaṃ gaccheyya
English Translation: “Why on earth am I going quickly?” “What if I were to go slowly?” [So] he would walk slowly.

Sutta Number: SNP23
Pali: bhagavā āyasmantaṃ rāhulaṃ imāhi gāthāhi abhiṇhaṃ ovadatī'ti
English Translation: The Blessed One frequently advises Venerable Rāhula with these verses.

Sutta Number: AN10.176
Pali: ime sattā haññantu vā bajjhantu vā ucchijjantu vā vinassantu vā mā vā ahesun’ti
English Translation: “May these beings be killed, be tied up, be chopped up, be destroyed, may they cease to exist!”

Sutta Number: SN22.79
Pali: ahaṃ kho eta’r’ahi rūpena khajjāmi. atītam’p’āhaṃ addhānaṃ evam’eva rūpena khajjiṃ
English Translation: Right now, I am truly being eaten by matter. In the past, I was also eaten by matter in the same way [as] at [this] time.

Sutta Number: AN4.186
Pali: kena nu kho, bhante, loko nīyati, kena loko parikassati
English Translation: Master, by what is the world lead? By what is the world dragged around?

Sutta Number: MN2.2
Pali: assutavā puthujjano na parimuccati jātiyā … , na parimuccati dukkhasmā’ti vadāmi
English Translation: I say: “An unlearned common person is not freed from birth. He is not completely freed from suffering."

Sutta Number: MN20
Pali: kiṃ nu kho ahaṃ saṇikaṃ gacchāmi?
English Translation: Why on earth do I walk slowly?

Sutta Number: SN46.3
Pali: passaddhakāyassa sukhino cittaṃ samādhiyati
English Translation: For one [whose] body [is] relaxed [and] who [is] at ease, the mind becomes composed.

Sutta Number: VIN PAT PC 73
Pali: ce bhikkhuṃ aññe bhikkhū jāneyyuṃ nisinnapubbaṃ iminā bhikkhunā dvattikkhattuṃ pātimokkhe uddissamāne, ko pana vādo bhiyyo’ti
English Translation: If other monks should know [about] [that] monk: “this monk has sat in two or three times before, while the disciplinary code was being recited, what to say of more [times]”.

Sutta Number: DHP201
Pali: jayaṃ veraṃ pasavati, dukkhaṃ seti parājito, upasanto sukhaṃ seti, hitvā jayaparājayaṃ.
English Translation: Victory generates ill-will, the conquered one sleeps with difficulty. The one who is at peace sleeps comfortably, having left behind victory and defeat.

Sutta Number: MN105
Pali: taṃ kiṃ maññasi, sunakkhatta, api nu tassa purisassa tasmiṃ vante puna bhottu'kamyatā assā'ti?
English Translation: What do you think, Sunakkhatta, after that man has vomited [some delectable food], would he have desire to eat [it] again?

Sutta Number: SN22.59
Pali: imasmiṃ ca pana veyyākaraṇasmiṃ bhaññamāne pañcavaggiyānaṃ bhikkhūnaṃ anupādāya āsavehi cittāni vimucciṃsū’ti.
English Translation: “And so, while this detailed exposition was being spoken, the minds of the group of five monks, by not grasping, were freed from taints.”

Sutta Number: MN75
Pali: aññe satte passāmi kāmesu avītarāge kāmataṇhāhi khajjamāne kāmapariḷāhena pariḍayhamāne kāme paṭisevante.
English Translation: I see other living beings not free from desire in regard to sensual pleasures, being devoured by cravings for sensual pleasures, being tormented by a burning passion for sensual objects, indulging in sensual pleasures.

Sutta Number: MN10.18
Pali: yaṃ loke piyarūpaṃ sātarūpaṃ, ettha esā taṇhā pahīyamānā pahīyati, ettha nirujjhamānā nirujjhati.
English Translation: For what [is] pleasant and enticing in the world this craving here is being abandoned, is abandoned, here it is ceasing, is ceased.

Sutta Number: MN22
Pali: evam'pi kho bhikkhu tehi bhikkhūhi samanuyuñjiyamāno samanugāhiyamāno samanubhāsiyamāno taṃ eva pāpakaṃ diṭṭhigataṃ thāmasā parāmāsā abhinivissa voharati
English Translation: Similarly, a monk being cross-questioned, interrogated, admonished by those monks, speaks adhering to that very wrong view, obstinately and with attachment.

Sutta Number: AN3.28
Pali: tayo ime, bhikkhave, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. katame tayo? gūthabhāṇī, pupphabhāṇī, madhubhāṇī.
English Translation: Monks, [there are] these three [types of] persons being found existing in the world. Which three? [Those] who lie, [those] who speak the truth, [and] [those] who talk sweetly.

Sutta Number: VIN PAT SA 13
Pali: tassa kho pāpakā samācārā dissanti c'eva suyyanti ca kulāni ca tena duṭṭhāni dissanti c'eva suyyanti ca
English Translation: His bad activities (lit: behaviors) are seen as well as heard [about] and the householders corrupted by him are seen as well as heard [about].

Sutta Number: UD10
Pali: tato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tena; yato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tena tato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tattha; yato tvaṃ, bāhiya, na tattha, tato tvaṃ, bāhiya, n'eva idha na huraṃ na ubhayam'antarena. es'ev'anto dukkhassā”ti.
English Translation: “Therefore, Bāhiya, you [won’t be] with that; because you [are] not with that, therefore, you, Bāhiya, [will] not [be] there. Since, you, Bāhiya, [are] not there, therefore, you, Bāhiya, [are] neither not here, nor in another world, nor in-between the two; just this is the end of suffering.”


Class 13 Extra

Sutta Number: SNP37
Pali: na munī gāmam’āgamma, kulesu sahasā care
English Translation: A monk, having arrived in the village, should not walk rashly among the households.

Sutta Number: APA3 (simpl)
Pali: jahanti purimaṃ nāmaṃ, buddhaputtā’ti ñāyanti
English Translation: They give up their former names and are known as "Buddha's sons"

Sutta Number: SNP8
Pali: mettaṃ ca sabbalokasmiṃ, mānasaṃ bhāvaye aparimāṇaṃ; uddhaṃ adho ca tiriyaṃ ca, asambādhaṃ, averaṃ, asapattaṃ. 
English Translation: One should cultivate a measureless and friendly mind for the entire world. Above, below and across; unobstructed, without enmity, without adversary.

Sutta Number: SNP8 (simpl)
Pali: diṭṭhā vā ye ca adiṭṭhā, ye сa dūre vasanti avidūre, bhūtā vā sambhavaṃ esī vā.
English Translation: Those that are seen or unseen and those that live far away or near, [those] [who] have been born or [those] seeking existence.

Sutta Number: KHP7
Pali: ayaṃ ca kho dakkhiṇā dinnā, saṅghamhi supatiṭṭhitā. dīgharattaṃ hitāya assa, ṭhānaso upakappati.
English Translation: And, indeed, this gift that has been given, [and is] well placed among the community of monks, could be for the benefit for a long time, and [also] is serving immediately.

Sutta Number: VIN PAT NP 10
Pali: catukkhattuṃ pañcakkhattuṃ chakkhattuṃ paramaṃ tuṇhībhūto uddissa tiṭṭhamāno taṃ cīvaraṃ abhinipphādeyya, iccetaṃ kusalaṃ
English Translation: [If after] (a monk) standing silently for the sake of [it] four times, five times, six times at the most, (a helper) should come up with that robe cloth, that is good.

Sutta Number: TH257 (simpl)
Pali: aññathā lokanāthamhi, tiṭṭhante puris'uttame. iriyā āsi bhikkhūnaṃ, aññathā dāni dissati.
English Translation: It was different when the Protector of the world, the supreme man, was still alive. Conduct of the monks was different; now [it] is seen [to be] otherwise.

Sutta Number: DHP265
Pali: yo ca sameti pāpāni, aṇuṃ thūlāni sabbaso, samitattā hi pāpānaṃ, samaṇo'ti pavuccati.
English Translation: He [is one] who makes subside evil deeds, [both] miniscule and large; because of the subsiding completely of evil deeds, he is indeed called “an ascetic.”

Sutta Number: SNP22
Pali: uṭṭhahatha nisīdatha, ko attho supitena vo, āturānaṃ hi kā niddā, sallaviddhānaṃ ruppataṃ.
English Translation: Arouse yourselves, sit up! What benefit [is there] for you by sleeping; What indeed is sleep for the afflicted, when they are being hurt, pierced by an arrow.

Sutta Number: AN9.11
Pali: taggha taṃ, bhikkhu, accayo accagamā yathābālaṃ yathāmūḷhaṃ yathāakusalaṃ, yo tvaṃ sāriputtaṃ asatā tucchā musā abhūtena abbhācikkhi
English Translation: O monk, truly you are at fault, you who so foolishly, so misguidedly, unjustly, baselessly, untruthfully and falsely slandered Sāriputta.
