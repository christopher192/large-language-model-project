Class 15 Exercises

Sutta Number: AN5.29
Pali: “pañcime, bhikkhave, caṅkame ānisaṃsā.
English Translation: “Monks, [there are] these five benefits of walking meditation.

Sutta Number: AN5.29
Pali: katame pañca?
English Translation: Which five?

Sutta Number: AN5.29
Pali: addhānakkhamo hoti, padhānakkhamo hoti, appābādho hoti, asitaṃ pītaṃ khāyitaṃ sāyitaṃ sammā pariṇāmaṃ gacchati, caṅkamādhigato samādhi ciraṭṭhitiko hoti.
English Translation: he is enduring of a long journey, he is enduring of exertion, he is healthy (lit: of not many illnesses), what has been eaten, drunk, chewed, [and] tasted [is] gets digested thoroughly. The stability of mind attained from walking meditation is long-lasting.

Sutta Number: AN5.29
Pali: ime kho, bhikkhave, pañca caṅkame ānisaṃsā”ti.
English Translation: Monks, these [are], indeed, the five benefits of walking meditation.”

Sutta Number: AN3.4
Pali: “tīhi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato bālo veditabbo. 
English Translation: Monks, one endowed with three qualities should be known as a fool.

Sutta Number: AN3.4
Pali: katamehi tīhi? 
English Translation: With which three?

Sutta Number: AN3.4
Pali: accayaṃ accayato na passati, accayaṃ accayato disvā yathā-dhammaṃ nappaṭikaroti, parassa kho pana accayaṃ desentassa yathādhammaṃ nappaṭiggaṇhāti.
English Translation: He does not see a fault as a fault. Having understood a fault as a fault, he does not redress it in accordance with the Doctrine. Indeed, when another person is pointing out a fault, he does not accept [it] in accordance with the Doctrine.

Sutta Number: AN3.4
Pali: imehi kho, bhikkhave, bhikkhave, tīhi dhammehi samannāgato bālo veditabbo.
English Translation: Certainly, monks, one possessing these three qualities should be known as a fool.

Sutta Number: AN5.198
Pali: “pañcahi, bhikkhave, aṅgehi samannāgatā vācā subhāsitā hoti, no dubbhāsitā, anavajjā ca ananuvajjā ca viññūnaṃ. 
English Translation: Monks, speech endowed with five factors is well-spoken and not ill-spoken; it is unblameable and not censurable by wise people.

Sutta Number: AN5.198
Pali: katamehi pañcahi?
English Translation: With which five?

Sutta Number: AN5.198
Pali: kālena ca bhāsitā hoti, saccā ca bhāsitā hoti, saṇhā ca bhāsitā hoti, attha-saṃhitā ca bhāsitā hoti, metta-cittena ca bhāsitā hoti.
English Translation: It is (speech) spoken at the proper time, it is spoken truthfully, it is spoken gently, it is spoken beneficially, it is spoken with a friendly mind.

Sutta Number: AN5.198
Pali: imehi kho, bhikkhave, pañcahi aṅgehi samannāgatā vācā subhāsitā hoti, no dubbhāsitā, anavajjā ca ananuvajjā ca viññūnan”ti.
English Translation: Indeed, monks, speech endowed with these five qualities is well-spoken and not ill-spoken; it is unblameable and not censurable by wise people.

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: “‘sammādiṭṭhi sammādiṭṭhī’ti, bhante, vuccati. kittāvatā nu kho, bhante, sammādiṭṭhi hotī”ti?
English Translation: Venerable sir, it is said, “right view, right view.” In what way, venerable sir, is there truly “right view?”

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: “dvayanissito kho ayaṃ, kaccāna, loko yebhuyyena — atthitaṃ ca eva n’atthitaṃ ca. 
English Translation: This world, Kaccāna, for the most part [is] dependent on a duality–on the idea of existence as well as the ideal of non-existence.

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: lokasamudayaṃ kho, kaccāna, yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya passato yā loke n’atthitā sā na hoti. 
English Translation: Certainly, for one seeing the source of the world as it truly is with correct understanding there is not that idea of non-existence in regard to the world.

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: lokanirodhaṃ kho, kaccāna, yathābhūtaṃ sammappaññāya passato yā loke atthitā sā na hoti.
English Translation: Indeed, for one seeing the cessation of the world as it really is with perfect wisdom, there is not that idea of existence.

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: upayupādānābhinivesavinibandho kho ayaṃ, kaccāna, loko yebhuyyena.
English Translation: Certainly, Kaccāna, this world is for the most part attached to engaging, grasping, and clinging. 

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: taṃ ce ayaṃ upayupādānaṃ cetaso adhiṭṭhānaṃ abhinives’ānusayaṃ na upeti na upādiyati n’ādhiṭṭhāti — ‘attā me’ti.
English Translation: But this (person) does not get involved with engagement and grasping, mental standpoint, adherence and underlying tendency, he does not grasp at that idea of non-existence; he does not take a stand “(this is) my self.”

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: ‘dukkhaṃ eva uppajjamānaṃ uppajjati, dukkhaṃ nirujjhamānaṃ nirujjhatī’ti na kaṅkhati na vicikicchati aparapaccayā ñāṇaṃ eva assa ettha hoti. 
English Translation: Arise is only arising suffering, cease is (only) ceasing suffering. He has no uncertainty, he has no doubt. He has knowledge in this case exclusively independent of others. 

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: ettāvatā kho, kaccāna, sammādiṭṭhi hoti.
English Translation: Truly, Kaccāna, to this extent there is right view.

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: “‘sabbaṃ atthī’ti kho, kaccāna, ayaṃ eko anto.
English Translation: “Everything exists,” Kaccāna: this is one extreme. 

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: ‘sabbaṃ n’atthī’ti ayaṃ dutiyo anto.
English Translation: “Everything does not exist”: this is the second extreme.

Sutta Number: SN12.15
Pali: ete te, kaccāna, ubho ante anupagamma majjhena tathāgato dhammaṃ deseti.
English Translation: Avoiding both these extremes, Kaccāna, the Tathāgata teaches the doctrine by means of the middle.
